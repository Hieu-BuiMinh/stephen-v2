The first four months of 2026 have already passed, and yet this year still feels like it is only just beginning.

My theme for the year is simple: **`finish what I started`** and continue doing what I've always done.

A simple sentence, but not always a simple thing to live by. I came into this year with that intention quietly sitting in the back of my mind, like a promise I made to myself and kept postponing. And if I am being honest, by the end of March, I still have not really begun the way I hoped I would.

## January - February

January and February slipped away in the familiar rhythm of preparing for Tết. Busy, scattered, full of small tasks and family moments — the kind of months that move quickly without asking for permission.

## March

Then came March, unfolding in a gentler tone.

March also gave me a moment worth remembering: on March 8, I attended several Dharma talks by master Giác Nguyên at Siêu Lý pagoda in District 6. It wasn't loud or dramatic, but it left a peaceful imprint on me — the kind of quiet grounding I didn't realize I needed.

I took a peaceful, memorable trip to Đà Lạt with my girlfriend — a few days wrapped in cool air, quiet streets, and the softness that only Đà Lạt seems to hold. It was the kind of trip that slows you down just enough to reflect, to breathe, to feel your life a little more clearly. A small escape, but a meaningful one.

## April

And then April arrived.

On April 7, I bought myself a chair for my workspace 🪑— something I had dreamed of for years, ever since I owned my very first PC. It may sound like a small thing to some people, but to me it felt like a milestone. A simple chair, yes, but also a reminder that dreams do not always arrive all at once. Sometimes they show up as practical, ordinary things after years of waiting.

There are still unfinished projects from past years. There are new ideas waiting to be shaped into something real. There is still a version of me that I have not fully become yet. But instead of treating that as a reason to feel behind, I want to treat it as a reason to keep going. So this is me, at the start of April, choosing motion over hesitation. Choosing consistency over pressure. Choosing to move forward, even if the pace is slow.

___By the end of this year, when I come back to the last line of this post, I want to see a checklist that tells a different story — one with more checkmarks than excuses.___

- A checklist of unfinished projects finally completed.
- A checklist of new projects that actually got started.
- A checklist of progress, discipline, and quiet persistence.

I started. I kept going. And I finished what I started 🎯
