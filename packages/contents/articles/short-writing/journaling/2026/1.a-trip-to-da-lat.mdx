## Da Lat, March 13-16 — A Journey Wrapped in Mist, Warm Food, and the Kind of Peace You Never Want to Leave

Some places are beautiful. And then there are places that stay with you. Da Lat was that kind of place for us.

Not just because of the pine trees, the cold weather, or the dreamy hills hidden beneath the mist — but because every hour of those four days felt like a small memory stitched gently into our hearts.

This was not simply a trip, it was a pause from the noise of life, a slow, healing chapter written in cool air, warm meals, soft rain, and the quiet One you love.

Our trip started at **11 PM on March 12**, departing from **Mien Tay Bus Station in Saigon**.

The city was still awake, street lights glowed against the dark sky, people were still moving through their routines, and somewhere in between all that familiar chaos, we were carrying something lighter inside us.

There is always something magical about starting a journey at night. The city slowly fades behind the bus window, and with every kilometer, your mind begins to drift away from work, responsibilities, and the everyday rhythm of life.

By the time we fell asleep, Da Lat was already waiting somewhere ahead.

## March 13 — A perfect beginning in the cold morning air

At **5 AM**, we arrived at **Da Lat Bus Station**. The first thing we felt was the cold. Around **15°C**, the kind of cold that immediately wakes you up; sharp, refreshing, and strangely comforting.

After arriving, we headed straight to our **homestay** to drop off our luggage and pick up the scooter. We had not checked in yet, but just leaving our bags there already made everything feel easier.

The trip had officially begun.

### Breakfast that felt like a warm hug

We had breakfast at **Bánh Căn Nhà Cổ**, a legendary place that has been around for **65 years**. And honestly, it was the perfect way to start Da Lat. The **xíu mại was unbelievably soft**, almost melting the moment it touched the tongue.

It did not just taste good. It _melted_ 🫠. The kind of softness that makes you pause for a second after the first bite.

<Image
	style={{ width: 300, height: 300 }}
	src="/assets/articles/other-topic/journaling/2026/1.a-trip-to-da-lat/01.png"
/>

And the bánh căn — fresh off the stove, still hot, slightly crispy on the edges, warm enough to fight off the chilly morning air. Sitting there in the cold weather, holding warm food in our hands, it felt like Da Lat was welcoming us in the gentlest way possible.

A perfect opening scene.

### Morning by Xuan Huong Lake

Then we wandered around **Xuan Huong Lake** for photos and check-ins. The lake in the morning looked calm and poetic. Still water, soft morning light, a city just beginning to wake up. We strolled along the lakeside, stopping at every beautiful corner that caught our eyes.

Then we headed toward the little **Hong Kong-style corner beside the market**, one of those iconic Da Lat photo spots that somehow always looks cinematic. After taking photos, we casually slipped into **Go!** to buy some ice cream.

Yes, ice cream in cold weather ☃️😅. And somehow, it was perfect. Laughing, taking silly photos, eating ice cream while the air was cold enough to make our fingers freeze a little.

those small moments often become the most unforgettable.

### Lunch with pine forest views

About **10:30 AM**, we headed to **Meraki Noodle House** for lunch.

Located only around **2 km from our homestay**, the place was already highly rated on Google Maps, but it still exceeded expectations. The noodles were delicious, but what truly made it memorable was the setting. The restaurant sat within a beautiful complex surrounded by **pine forest views and sloping mountain roads**.

Da Lat has a way of making even lunch feel cinematic.

- Good food.
- Cool air.
- A quiet forest outside.

Everything felt slower here.

At **2 PM**, we finally checked in at **Vườn Đom Đóm**. The room was cozy, peaceful, and exactly what we needed. A place to rest, recharge, and prepare for one of the most beautiful moments of the trip.

### The sunset we still talk about

Around **4:00 to 6:00 PM**, we went to **Tiệm Trà Phụng**. This was, without question, one of the best moments of the entire trip. We intentionally came early to order the **roasted tea set** and settle in before sunset. And it was worth every minute.

### Night market comfort food

From **6:00 to 8:00 PM**, we walked around **Da Lat Night Market**.

First stop: **grilled rice paper right at the roundabout area**. And wow, so good.

Crispy, savory, slightly smoky, and absolutely addictive.

Then came **avocado ice cream**. Rich, creamy, and incredibly satisfying. We also considered trying soy milk and cheese yogurt, but the yogurt turned out a little too sweet for our taste.

By **8:00 PM**, we stopped by **Lata** to pick up some gifts. And by then, we were honestly too full to continue snacking. Then we went back to the homestay and slept early. The kind of sleep that only comes after a perfect day.

## March 14 — Streams, forest views, and cold night barbecue

The next morning started at **8 AM**. We woke up early, packed our things, and moved into a new room called **Chồi Xuân**.

By **10:15 AM**, we headed out for one of the most scenic destinations of the trip: **Kẻng Đà Lạt**. This place was amazing. A café by the stream with lunch service, beautiful views, and even cats 😺. There was something incredibly healing about this place.

Later, we walked around **Lam Vien Square**, parking at Go! and strolling to help digest all the barbecue. Of course, we also stopped by Go! again for instant noodles and **Tràng Tiền ice cream** before heading home early.

## March 15 — Quiet rain and the place we loved the most

This day started slower. And honestly, beautifully so. Around **8:00 to 10:00 AM**, we stayed in bed longer than planned. The cool weather made sleeping feel dangerously comfortable. Sometimes the best luxury in Da Lat is simply not rushing.

Later, we headed out to **Lưng Đồi** café. The view was peaceful and soft, exactly what we needed for a slow morning.

### The place we loved most

From **1:30 to 2:00 PM**, we took the **cable car to Trúc Lâm Zen Monastery**.

This was, without question, the place we loved the most. We spent hours here, not doing much, just sitting, breathing, watching the rain fall softly. The light drizzle, the silence, the vast open space, and the peaceful atmosphere made it feel like the outside world had completely disappeared.

- No noise.
- No pressure.
- No city chaos.

Just peace 😇. It was deeply relaxing. The kind of place that makes you feel emotionally lighter after leaving. We already know we will come back again in the future.

## March 16 — Leaving, but not really

In the morning we packed everything, checked our luggage carefully, and returned the scooter.

There is always a strange feeling on the last morning of a trip. You are physically getting ready to leave, but emotionally, a part of you is still staying.

At **10 AM**, we boarded the bus back to Saigon. Da Lat slowly disappeared behind the windows.

- The pine trees.
- The slopes.
- The mist.
- The quiet roads.

But somehow, it never really felt like goodbye. Because some places do not leave you when the trip ends.
